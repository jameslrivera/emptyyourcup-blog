There's a saying I come back to often: "Your team will never outwork, out-care, or out-discipline their leader."

## The Mirror Effect

Teams reflect their leaders. If you show up late, your team will show up late. If you cut corners, your team will cut corners. If you stay calm under pressure, your team learns to stay calm under pressure.

This isn't about being perfect. It's about being consistent.

## What This Looks Like in Practice

- Admit when you're wrong — publicly
- Do the work you ask others to do
- Hold yourself to the same standards (or higher) that you set for others
- Show up prepared

## The Compound Effect

Leading by example doesn't produce instant results. It compounds over time. Six months of consistent behavior builds more trust than any motivational speech ever could.

People don't follow titles. They follow people they trust. And trust is built through action, not words.
